Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy is an innovative regenerative treatment that harnesses the healing power of your body’s own blood. A small sample of blood is collected and processed to concentrate platelets rich in growth factors. These powerful proteins are then carefully injected into targeted areas of the skin or scalp to promote natural healing and tissue regeneration. PRP therapy has become one of the most sought-after treatments for individuals seeking natural, non-surgical solutions for aging skin and hair thinning. PRP Treatment Process

Step 1 – Consultation & Assessment

Our dermatologist performs a detailed evaluation of your skin or hair condition, discusses your goals, and creates a personalized treatment plan.

Step 2 – Blood Collection & Plasma Separation

A small amount of blood is collected and placed into a specialized centrifuge that separates platelet-rich plasma from other blood components.

Step 3 – PRP Injection for Skin/Hair

The concentrated PRP is carefully injected into targeted areas of the skin or scalp to stimulate natural healing, collagen production, and follicular regeneration.

Step 4 – Recovery & Results Timeline

Patients typically experience minimal downtime and can return to normal activities quickly. Visible improvements develop gradually as the body’s natural regenerative processes take effect.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is PRP treatment painful?

Most patients experience only mild discomfort. Topical numbing solutions may be used to enhance comfort during the procedure.

How many sessions are required?

The number of sessions varies depending on individual goals and conditions. Most patients benefit from a series of 3–6 sessions for optimal results.

Is PRP safe for all skin types?

Yes. Since PRP uses your own blood, it is generally considered safe for most skin types and hair restoration candidates when performed by qualified professionals.

How soon will I see results?

Results vary among individuals. Skin rejuvenation improvements may become visible within a few weeks, while hair regrowth results typically develop over several months.

Can PRP be combined with other treatments?

Yes. PRP therapy is often combined with advanced dermatological procedures to enhance overall treatment outcomes.
